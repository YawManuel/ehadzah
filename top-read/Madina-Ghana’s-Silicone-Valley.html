Madina, Ghana’s Silicone Valley

Author: Hadzah, Emmanuel. 12th July, 2022.
Is now a boom in the software world with lot and lot of tech companies, from Giant tech companies to Unicorn level to numerous of coding bootcamps in the world now. Does Ghana have it share of such tech establishment? Let’s take our travelling lenses and world map and have a look at our new Unicorn tech Community in the world, Madina.
It all started with the yahoo boys with the springing of internet cafes all over the place. I can still remember my first email, which was a Yahoo mail. How fun and important you were if you own an email during those days. Computers were becoming popular in the Madina communities with internet cafes opening up everywhere, where its number one patronizes were the yahoo boys. People went into the cafes to chat with clients through Yahoo Messenger, there wasn’t much learning done on the internet by users by then. This was around 2004-2007 where most schools did not have computers, also it was very difficult to get a home with a computer. So, schools will take their students to large internet cafes to have a feel of it and learn how to use it, my first computer usage experience was when our school took us to an internet café, this was when I was in primary. The internet cafes were really the genesis and the driving force of computer literacy in the communities which has become now Ghana’s Silicon Valley.
Madina being the hub of great schools, things started to change from 2008 when junior high schools and senior high schools started having their own computer labs; computers started to be available in most homes; introduction of smart phones and the springing up of special form of internet cafes for training students how to use computers. These changes took the crown from the yahoo boys to the academicians as the most computer users. My family and I left Madina in 2010, so I missed 9 years of personal experience when the table turned in 2008, though I seldom visit Madina to observe the rampant development in the communities in Madina.
In University, I had little hope for the country in terms of digital technology development. But not until I completed school and started applying for tech roles and realise my tech job hunting radar was always pointing to Madina. Whats is happening in my Madina? So, I did ground research of the number of tech companies, coding bootcamps, startups, and I found out that, the lost hope I was having can be found in Madina, my Madina. Is incredible to see how just within 9 years Madina has turn into a tech hub, once a community with internet cafes all over the place led by yahoo boys is now a tech city with tech companies, coding bootcamps, fintech companies, startups led by Academicians, technocrats, students, investors. Most of these companies are software-oriented companies, generally Ghana don’t have big computer hardware tech companies, but mostly repairs and some assembling of laptops. Circle Tip Toe Lane being the number one spot to get all your computer brands and all kind of Computer accessories to buy, where they also assemble laptops. Madina is catching up with hardware related tech companies, but for now, is all software-oriented companies.
With the future of the expansion of Ghana’s silicon Valley, Koforidua in Eastern region and Ho in the Volta region looks promising. For this is when the tech companies in Madina starts expanding and need real-estate to expand the company workspace.
